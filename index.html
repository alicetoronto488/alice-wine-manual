Wine Fundamentals

At Alice, wine isn't just a beverage — it's part of the experience. We are an upscale casual steakhouse and late-night dining destination in the heart of Little Italy. Our guests expect guidance, and the staff member who confidently leads them through the wine list earns trust, bigger tabs, and better tips.

The Five Families

Sparkling — Think celebration, effervescence, first impressions. Offer it the moment guests sit down, especially for special occasions. Champagne and Prosecco open wallets and set the tone for the entire meal.

White — Crisp, refreshing, versatile. Ranges from bone-dry Sauvignon Blanc to buttery Chardonnay. Whites shine with our seafood dishes, pastas, and lighter appetizers. Always serve chilled.

Rosé — The patio wine. Beautiful colour, approachable flavour. Rosé thrives in the warm months and pairs effortlessly with Alice's shareables and salads.

Red — The backbone of our list and the soul of a steakhouse. From smooth Merlots to bold Cabernets, reds pair with our Tomahawks, Striploins, and heartier mains. This is where the big upsell opportunities live.

Key Vocabulary

Dry vs. Sweet — Most of our wines are dry. The Moselland Riesling is off-dry. Know this so you can steer guests toward their preference.

Body — Light body (like our Pinot Grigio) feels light in the mouth. Full body (like Cabernet) feels rich. Match heavy dishes with full-bodied wines.

Tannins — That drying sensation in bold reds. Tannins are softened by fat and protein — which is why a tannic Cabernet pairs perfectly with our 28oz Tomahawk.

Perfect Pairings

Wine pairing isn't complicated. Match intensity with intensity:

  • Light dishes (salads, seafood, pasta) → Light & crisp wines (Pinot Grigio, Sauvignon Blanc)
  • Rich dishes (cream sauces, butter, cheese) → Medium to full wines (Chardonnay, Merlot)
  • Beef & red meat → Bold reds (Cabernet, our Super Tuscan)
  • Celebration or uncertainty → Champagne or Prosecco (always safe, always welcome)

Upsell System

The goal isn't to push expensive bottles. The goal is to match guests with wines that elevate their meal.

Step 1: Listen. Ask "Are you ordering beef, seafood, or a lighter dish?" Their choice tells you everything.

Step 2: Suggest the right tier. Start with a mid-range option. If they seem comfortable, float the premium.

Step 3: Use the story. Don't say "This is expensive." Say "This pairs unforgettably with your Tomahawk" or "This is personal favorite."

Step 4: Respect the decision. If they order house Prosecco, treat it like they ordered Dom. Your enthusiasm matters more than the price tag.

Live Scenarios

Scenario 1: "I don't know much about wine..."
Guest: "I'm not really a wine person. Just get me something red."
You: "Perfect. What are you ordering for food tonight? Beef, seafood, or something lighter?"
Their food choice, not their wine knowledge, is your guide. Pair to the dish, not their ego.
Scenario 2: Upselling the Premium
Guest: "I'll take the Merlot."
You: "Great choice — our Caymus Cabernet is on special right now, and it pairs even better with what you're ordering. Would you like to try that instead?"
Notice: You acknowledge their choice first. This builds trust, then the upsell lands.
Scenario 3: The Celebration
Guest: "We're celebrating an engagement tonight."
You: "Congratulations! There's nothing quite like Dom Pérignon for a moment like this. Should I bring that?"
Don't ask if they want Champagne. Tell them what Champagne you recommend. Own your expertise.
Scenario 4: Budget Concern
Guest: "Something inexpensive, please."
You: "Of course. The Mionetto Prosecco is our best value — it's festive, delicious, and pairs with everything. Or if you're ordering beef, our Merlot is solid and approachable."
Never make someone feel bad about budget. Just steer them to the best option at their price point.
Scenario 5: The Committed Guest
Guest: "I'm ordering the Tomahawk. What do you recommend with that?"
You: "The Caymus Cabernet was made for that steak. Full-bodied, tannic — it grips the fat and makes every bite better."
When someone trusts you, give them confidence. Use technical language — it builds authority.

Golden Rules

1. TASTE MATTERS MORE THAN PRICE

A $20 Prosecco that makes the guest happy beats a $100 Burgundy they don't enjoy.

2. CHAMPAGNE IS ALWAYS THE ANSWER

Celebration? Uncertainty? First course? Champagne or Prosecco will never disappoint.

3. MATCH FOOD, NOT THE GUEST

Forget what they ordered last time. Ask what they're eating tonight, then pair to that.

4. CONFIDENCE SELLS

If you seem uncertain, they'll feel uncertain. Know your bottles, own your recommendations.

5. NEVER DISMISS A CHOICE

If they order house wine, treat it like they ordered Dom. Your professionalism matters more than their decision.

6. BIG PLATES = BIG WINES

28oz Tomahawk? Striploin? Recommend bold reds. Light wines get lost with heavy beef.

7. SERVE WITH PURPOSE

Bring wine like it's meant to be there. Make the moment feel special, because it is.
