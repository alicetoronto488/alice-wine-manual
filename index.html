Perfect Pairings

Match intensity with intensity:

  • Light dishes (salads, seafood, pasta) to Light wines (Pinot Grigio, Sauvignon Blanc)
  • Rich dishes (cream, butter, cheese) to Medium/Full wines (Chardonnay, Merlot)
  • Beef and red meat to Bold reds (Cabernet, Super Tuscan, Malbec)
  • Celebration or uncertainty to Champagne or Prosecco (always safe)

Upsell System

Three-Step Upsell:

1. Listen: Ask "What are you ordering — beef, seafood, or something lighter?" Their choice tells you everything.

2. Suggest the right tier: Start with a mid-range option. If comfortable, float the premium. Lead with Italian and French.

3. Use the story: Don't say "This is expensive." Say "This pairs unforgettably with your Tomahawk" or "This is our house pride."

The Golden Principle: Respect their decision. If they order house wine, treat it like Dom. Your enthusiasm matters more than price.

Live Scenarios

Scenario 1: I Don't Know Much About Wine
Guest: "I'm not really a wine person. Just get me something red."
You: "Perfect. What are you ordering for food tonight? Beef, seafood, or something lighter?"
Their food choice, not wine knowledge, is your guide. Pair to the dish.
Scenario 2: Upselling from Mid-Range
Guest: "I'll take the Solo Super Tuscan."
You: "Great choice — that's our house pride. It pairs beautifully with what you're ordering. Should I bring that?"
Acknowledge their choice first. Then the upsell lands.
Scenario 3: The Celebration
Guest: "We're celebrating an engagement tonight."
You: "Congratulations! There's nothing quite like Dom Pérignon for a moment like this. Should I bring that?"
Don't ask if they want Champagne. Tell them what you recommend. Own your expertise.
Scenario 4: Belaire Easy Push
Guest: Your group looks like you're celebrating tonight.
You: "Belaire Rosé is perfect for this vibe — festive, fun, pairs with everything. Can I grab a bottle?"
Read the room. Belaire is the easiest sell on the list.
Scenario 5: Italian and French Priority
Guest: "What's your best red?"
You: "For premium reds, we have two crown jewels: our Solo Super Tuscan ($150) — Italian, bold, our house signature — or the Ornellaia ($900) — if you want to go all-in. Both are magnificent."
Lead with Italian and French. Both are fully stocked and have the best margins.

Golden Rules

1. Push Italian and French First

California wines are "While Supplies Last." Lead premium upsells with Italian (Solo, Ornellaia, Alice Verdeca) and French wines.

2. Belaire for Celebration

$150. No wine knowledge required. Just read the room — birthdays, groups, late-night energy.

3. Match Food, Not the Guest

Forget what they ordered last time. Ask what they're eating tonight, then pair to that.

4. Confidence Sells

If you seem uncertain, they feel uncertain. Know your wines, own your recommendations.

5. Acknowledge Before Upselling

If they order something, acknowledge it first. "Great choice" opens the door for upselling.

6. Tell Stories

"Our namesake wine" (Alice Verdeca). "Our house pride" (Solo). "Crown jewel" (Dom, Ornellaia). Stories sell better than prices.

7. Respect the Decision

If they order house wine, treat it like Dom. Your professionalism matters more than their choice.
